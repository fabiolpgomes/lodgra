# AI Execution Protocol

**Project:** Brazil Vacation Rental Intelligence Platform  
**Version:** 1.0

---

# Mission

Validate, design and execute the development of an AI-first operational intelligence platform for the Brazilian short-term rental market.

The platform must solve operational and financial problems that current PMS solutions do not solve adequately.

**Primary Objective**

> Become the AI Operations Layer for vacation rental managers instead of another PMS.

---

# Strategic Principles

- Solve operational pain before building features.
- Validate every hypothesis with paying customers.
- Prefer intelligence over automation.
- Integrate with existing PMS platforms whenever possible.
- Every feature must reduce cost, increase revenue, or save time.
- Build only after commercial validation.

---

# Success Metrics

The project is successful when:

- Target users confirm the pain is real.
- Customers are willing to pay before software is fully developed.
- The platform demonstrably improves operational efficiency.
- AI recommendations generate measurable business value.

# Workstreams

## Agent A — Market Research

### Objective

Validate whether the pain exists.

### Tasks

- Identify the largest operational problems.
- Map current workflows.
- Identify existing software.
- Interview property managers.

### Deliverable

`MARKET-RESEARCH.md`

### Acceptance Criteria

- Evidence that the top operational pains are recurring.
- At least 20 qualified interviews completed.

---

## Agent B — Competitor Intelligence

### Objective

Understand the Brazilian competitive landscape.

### Tasks

Analyze:

- PMS platforms
- Channel Managers
- Operations software
- AI-enabled products

Identify:

- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Feature gaps
- Pricing
- Positioning

### Deliverable

`COMPETITOR-ANALYSIS.md`

### Acceptance Criteria

- Complete competitor matrix.
- Clear opportunity map.

---

## Agent C — Customer Discovery

### Objective

Validate willingness to pay.

### Tasks

Conduct 20–30 interviews.

Capture:

- Biggest frustrations
- Current tools
- Manual work
- Financial impact
- Purchase process
- Budget
- Buying triggers

### Deliverable

`CUSTOMER-DISCOVERY.md`

### Acceptance Criteria

- Minimum of 5 qualified prospects willing to run a paid pilot.

---

## Agent D — Pain Prioritization

### Objective

Rank opportunities.

### Evaluation Matrix

Each pain must receive scores for:

- Frequency
- Financial impact
- Time wasted
- Emotional frustration
- Automation potential
- Revenue opportunity

### Deliverable

`PAIN-MAP.md`

### Acceptance Criteria

- Top 10 validated pains ranked by opportunity.

---

## Agent E — Solution Architect

### Objective

Design solutions only for validated pains.

Each solution must define:

- Problem
- Current workaround
- AI opportunity
- Expected ROI
- MVP scope
- Success metric

### Deliverable

`SOLUTION-BACKLOG.md`

---

## Agent F — Concierge MVP

### Deliverable

`CONCIERGE-MVP.md`

# Definition of Done

The project is complete only when:

- The market pain has been validated.
- Customers have paid for the solution.
- The MVP solves one critical problem exceptionally well.
- Product direction is driven by evidence rather than assumptions.
- The roadmap is based on validated demand rather than feature parity with competitors.

---

# Core Philosophy

> Do not build software because a feature is missing.

> Build software because a measurable business problem exists, customers recognize it, and they are willing to pay for a better solution.

The AI platform must become the operational intelligence layer that property managers rely on every day to make better decisions, reduce costs, improve guest satisfaction, and increase profitability.
